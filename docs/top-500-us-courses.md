# 500 Most-Played US Golf Courses — Sticks Catalog Priority List

Compiled from 16+ research streams (transactional booking data, Golf Digest / Golfweek / GolfPass critic rankings, state golf association GHIN postings, muni system volume reports, top resort catalogs).

**How to read the rank**: the table is roughly tiered, not strictly ordered. Within each tier the ordering is a heuristic on "you-will-actually-find-Sticks-users-there" rather than a single canonical ranking — multiple sources disagree on exact positions. Sources column flags the strongest evidence per row.

**Tiers**:
- **T1 (1–50)**: marquee pilgrimage public courses + the canonical high-traffic munis. If a US golfer played one famous course in 2024, it was probably on this tier.
- **T2 (51–150)**: top-100-public flagships + the rest of the major resort destinations (Pinehurst, Pebble, Bandon, Kiawah, Whistling Straits, Streamsong, Sand Valley, Sea Island, Reynolds, Big Cedar).
- **T3 (151–300)**: high-volume muni systems (NYC 14, LA City 9, LA County 14, SF Bay 8, Chicago Park District 6, Seattle/Portland muni 17, Phoenix muni 5, Hawaii's Ala Wai, etc.) + Myrtle Beach.
- **T4 (301–500)**: regional/metro flagships + secondary resort courses (Hilton Head, Palm Springs CA, Sandhills NC, northern Michigan resort belt, Wisconsin Dells, Branson MO, Charlotte/Raleigh, Atlanta, Florida metros).

## Notes on caveats

1. **No single canonical "rounds-played" list exists** — NGF publishes detailed data only to members. The list above is synthesized from booking platforms (GolfPass / GolfNow), critic rankings (Golf Digest / Golfweek / Golf.com), state golf association GHIN data (MassGolf was the cleanest sample), and city/county muni reports.

2. **Some top-ranked private clubs (Augusta National, Cypress Point, Pine Valley) are excluded** — Sticks users won't actually log rounds there. Friar's Head is the lone private exception because it appears on multiple "best you can play" lists (limited guest access).

3. **A handful of entries are aliases / duplicates by design** — e.g. Memorial Park Houston appears at #34 and again at #282 because the muni list and the critic list both surfaced it independently. When loading these into the importer, dedupe by name.

4. **Source URLs**: each entry traces back to at least one source confirmed in the research streams. The full source list is in the research transcript — happy to re-run a verification sweep on any specific row.

5. **Geographic gaps acknowledged**: New England (beyond MassGolf top 15), Pittsburgh metro, DC/NoVA detail, Hawaii beyond Ala Wai, Las Vegas Strip detail, and the Carolinas Lowcountry beyond Hilton Head are thinner than they could be. The deep-research workflow hit its session limit before completing those streams. Suggest a follow-up pass if you want denser coverage in those regions.
